Introduction to Payday loans

A payday loan is a short-term, small-dollar loan that you can get when you need cash in a hurry. You pay back the loan plus interest when your next paycheck arrives. Payday loans are only for people who expect to be paid within two weeks and have an account with direct deposit or online payments set up through their employer.

Payday loans are designed for emergencies—you might use one to cover an unexpected expense like a car repair, or if you’re between paychecks and need some cash for groceries or rent. The amount of time between applying for your payday loan and receiving it depends on how quickly the lender can process your information and send funds electronically into your bank account (usually no more than 24 hours). Once you receive the money from the lender, it will automatically transfer out of your account after several days—often one week later—so make sure there’s enough money in there to cover both this payment plus anything else that comes due during that period. If not, consider asking family members or friends if they would be willing to help out by covering this expense until next week’s paycheck hits!
